A Materials Comparison In Minimizing Head Injury Severity from Falls

Reducing Head Injuries From Falls

Background

Globally, children falling off chairs is a significant problem that can result in life threatening or life changing injuries. Students today do not have the balance and core strength seen in the past. This, along with a sharp increase in fidgeting largely due to attention and sensory disorders, has led to a rise in the number of children falling out of classroom chairs in recent years. Furthermore, children falling out of chairs landing on rigid flooring can result in severe injuries. The most common injuries of a fall in children are head injuries, which can even be life-threatening.

Rationale

The most effective flooring material to decrease injury severity caused by falls in school should be identified and incorporated into classrooms. Individuals that may benefit from this research include elementary school children, particularly students with attention or sensory disorders, as well as other disabilities. This study will also help to expand current knowledge of impact-absorbing materials.
